Mean Household Economics of Malaysia

Household economics in Malaysia reflects the financial well-being and living standards of families across different regions. Over the past few decades, Malaysia has achieved steady income growth and poverty reduction through industrialisation, urban expansion, and improved education access.

Behind Malaysia’s steady rise in income lies a diverse story — where some states thrive while others face persistent inequality.
Overview

Understanding the Indicators

Each indicator highlights a key aspect of Malaysia’s socioeconomic landscape.

  • Mean Income — average household earnings.
  • Mean Expenditure — average household spending patterns.
  • Poverty Rate — proportion of families living below the national poverty line.

Economic Disparities Across Malaysia

The 2022 data highlights Malaysia’s persistent economic divide between urbanized and rural regions. Western Peninsular Malaysia, particularly Kuala Lumpur, Selangor, and Penang, continues to lead with higher mean household incomes. This prosperity stems from established industrial sectors, service hubs, and robust infrastructure.

In contrast, East Malaysia, especially Sabah and Sarawak, shows slower income growth due to logistical challenges, limited industrial activity and dependency on primary industries such as palm oil, timber, and petroleum. Although poverty rates have declined nationally, a significant economic inequality gap still exists in these states.

States like Johor and Melaka have shown steady progress, benefiting from cross-border trade and manufacturing expansion. Meanwhile, northern states such as Kedah and Perlis maintain moderate income levels with slower economic advancement.

Income

Understanding the Income Trends

  • 2007–2014: Income rose steadily while poverty rates declined, reflecting post-industrial growth.
  • 2016: Despite modest income growth, poverty rate spiked due to introduction of GST and inflation pressures.
  • 2020–2022: Both income dropped and poverty rose, highlighting COVID-19's direct economic impact.

Overall, high income usually aligns with lower poverty, but GST and COVID-19 disrupts this pattern and widen disparities.

Income Ranking Dynamics

Despite national growth, the ranking chart shows persistent regional inequality.

  • Kuala Lumpur consistently ranks top, showing sustained economic strength.
  • Putrajaya and Selangor maintain high income levels with minor shifts.
  • Johor shows steady progress, reflecting strong industrial growth.
  • Perlis and Kedah remain in lower ranks but show gradual improvement.

Central Malaysia (Kuala Lumpur, Selangor, Putrajaya) consistently dominate income rankings, while East Malaysia remains at the bottom despite gradual improvement.

Expenditure

Household Spending Patterns

Malaysia's overall household spending remained relatively stable between 2016 to 2019, then experiencing slight decline in 2022, reflecting impact of COVID-19 pandemic on Malaysia's economy.

The Central Region recorded the highest mean expenditure, supported by higher living costs and income levels in urbanised states like Kuala Lumpur and Selangor.

In contrast, East Malaysia and East Coast regions like Kelantan and Terengganu showed lovwer average expenditure, aligning with their generally lower income levels and more rural composition.

In terms of expenditure categories, Housing & Utilities contributed to the largest share of spending, followed by Restaurants & Accomodations, Food & Beverages and Transport. This indicates that hosuehold budgets are mainly dominated by essential spending while expenditures on Recreation & Culture, Clothing & Footwear and other non essentials remain comparatively smaller.

Overall, this reflects how Malaysian households prioritize necessities despite ongoing cost-of-living pressure and the gradual economic recovery.
