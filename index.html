Mean Household Economics of Malaysia by State

Household economics in Malaysia reflects the financial well-being and living standards of families across different regions. Over the past few decades, Malaysia has achieved steady income growth and poverty reduction through industrialisation, urban expansion, and improved education access.

Behind Malaysia’s steady rise in income lies a diverse story — where some states thrive while others face persistent inequality.

Malaysia’s Socioeconomic Landscape

Understanding the Indicators

Each indicator highlights a key aspect of Malaysia’s socioeconomic landscape.

  • Mean Income — average household earnings.
  • Mean Expenditure — average household spending patterns.
  • Poverty Rate — proportion of families living below the national poverty line.

Exploring Malaysia’s Income & Poverty Landscape

Understanding the Income Trends

  • 2007–2014: Steady income growth and declining poverty rates, reflecting post-industrial growth.
  • 2016: Poverty rate increased due to introduction of GST and inflation pressures.
  • 2020–2022: COVID-19 led to income decrease and renewed poverty rise.
